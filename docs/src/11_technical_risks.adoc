== Risks and Technical Debts
Considering the recent loss of a team member, the team's tendency towards end-of-sprint workload accumulation, challenges with branch merging due to testing, and the adoption of new technologies, the following are key risks and potential areas of technical debt that require attention (Relevance from 1(low) to 3(high)):

[cols="1,1,2,2"]
|===
| Category & Item | Relevance | Considerations | Potential Mitigation
| Workflow Risk (End-of-sprint spikes) | 2 | Accumulating work at the end of a sprint leads to rushed implementations, bugs, and "quick fixes" that introduce technical debt. | Implement strict "Work in Progress" (WIP) limits, encourage Continuous Integration (CI), and prioritize task completion early in the sprint.
| Code Integration Risk (Merge conflicts) | 2 | Frequent conflicts disrupt flow and signal gaps in test coverage or architectural understanding. Hastily resolved conflicts hide technical debt. | Enforce stricter test requirements before merging, adopt a robust branching strategy, and investigate root causes of recurring failures.
| Technology Adoption (React, MongoDB, Rust) | 2 | Limited experience can lead to suboptimal implementations, performance bottlenecks, or maintainability challenges. | Conduct internal knowledge-sharing sessions, focus code reviews on best practices, and dedicate spike time for deep-dive learning.
| Implicit Knowledge (Bus factor) | 1 | Critical modules rely on specific individuals, creating a bottleneck and a risk to long-term maintainability. | Maintain up-to-date documentation of key functionalities, use pair programming for complex modules, and document decisions via ADRs.
|===
